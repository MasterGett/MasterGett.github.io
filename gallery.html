Pumpkin Variety Gallery

On our farm, we annually grow an assortment of over 20 kinds of pumpkins and other squash. Over the years we have tried over 50 varieties and found that some grow especially well in the Pacific Northwest climate (eg. Sweet Meat), though some, unfortunately, don't produce quite as well or fail to ripen fully (eg. Kikuza). All pumpkins grown on our farm are food quality, and can be used for cooking purposes when their time as autumn decor is finished. Additionally, many of the varieties we grow specialize in certain aspects of cooking (eg. the Long Island Cheese is especially good for pies and baking).

Pumpkin Varieties

Below is an alphabetically-arranged compilation of the many pumpkins and squash we have grown at Creek House Farm over the years. Each entry will have an image of the pumpkin or squash when ripe, the common name(s) of the variety, and a brief description of general appearance and cooking uses. Some may also be followed by additional historical/trivial information.

Notice: Please be aware that not all of the varieties listed below will be available for purchase during your visit to Creek House Farm (reasons may include not being planted in May, or selling out quickly in October).
